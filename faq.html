FAQ for Faculty & Administrators

Academic

Who are these programs for?

Our programs are designed for U.S. universities offering sport-related coursework. We work directly with faculty and study abroad offices to create short-term, faculty-led programs that integrate seamlessly into existing academic structures.

What experience do you have with these programs?

We've been organizing international, sport-focused study abroad programs for over 10 years. Our founder has worked with a range of U.S. institutions, delivering academically aligned and logistically seamless experiences across Europe and South America. We understand what faculty, students, and administrators needâ€”and we deliver with professionalism, consistency, and care.

What subjects or themes can we focus on?

Each program is custom-built around your course content. Focus areas include:

  • Sport management and administration
  • The European Model of Sport
  • Governance and leadership in sport
  • Kinesiology, sport psychology, and more

Do students receive academic credit?

Yes. Credit is awarded by your institution. We work with faculty to align site visits and activities with the course syllabus and learning outcomes.

What is the university's role in the program?

Your university's study abroad office and academic department remain responsible for student enrollment, teaching the class before departure, academic credit, and pre-departure safety briefings. Sport Study Abroad works in close partnership with your institution to ensure all travel logistics and on-site support are seamlessly integrated with your university's requirements and processes.

Logistics

Where can the programs take place?

We have organized programs in a wide range of countries and have built an extensive network across Europe as well as in select countries in Asia, Central, and South America. Destinations are selected based on their relevance to your academic goals and their unique contributions to global sport culture. Popular locations include:

  • United Kingdom
  • The Netherlands
  • Spain
  • France
  • Italy
  • Switzerland
  • Greece

We're happy to recommend the best location based on your course focus.

How long are the programs?

Programs typically run 10 to 14 days, scheduled around Spring Break, Maymester, Summer Sessions, or December Break.

What's included in the program package?

We handle the full logistics. Each program typically includes:

  • Hotel or student accommodation
  • Ground transportation
  • Curated site visits and expert speakers
  • Cultural activities
  • Select meals
  • On-site coordination and 24/7 support
  • Faculty leader travel included in student pricing

Can we customize the itinerary?

Yes. All programs are fully customized in collaboration with faculty. We adapt the itinerary, site visits, and themes to match your course goals, student interests, and budget.

Can this be offered during December Break or Winter Session?

Yes. In addition to Spring Break, Maymester, and Summer Sessions, we also support programs during December or Winter Break, depending on academic calendars and destination availability.
