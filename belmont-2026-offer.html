Tentative Itinerary Overview: UK Sport Industry Introduction

Below is a proposed daily itinerary, designed for clarity and ease of understanding. Each day highlights key activities and experiences. The final itinerary will be meticulously customized in close collaboration with you.

Total Program Duration: 12 days / 11 nights
Proposed Travel Window: Thursday, May 14, 2026 - Monday, May 25, 2026
Faculty: Professor Ted Peetz (Sport and Society), Professor Michelle Shaw (Sport Communication), Professor Stephen Hankil Shin (Mega Events)

London, England (May 14 - May 17, 2026)

Day 1 (Thursday, May 14): Arrival & Cricket Immersion

  • Arrive at Heathrow Airport.
  • Visit Lord's Cricket Ground: participate in a cricket workshop and take a facility tour.
  • Begin a comprehensive walking city tour.

Day 2 (Friday, May 15): Wimbledon & Sport Business

  • Explore Wimbledon: attend a lecture on the Business of Wimbledon, take a tennis facility tour, and visit the Wimbledon museum.

Day 3 (Saturday, May 16): London at Leisure

  • Enjoy a free day for independent exploration in London.

Day 4 (Sunday, May 17): Live UK Sport Experience

  • Attend a major sport game or event, such as a Premier League football match (e.g., Arsenal v Burnley, Chelsea v Tottenham, Hotspur v Sunderland, Everton v Manchester United, or equivalent game depending on schedule availability).
  • Travel by train to Manchester.

Manchester, England (May 18 - May 20, 2026 including Liverpool day trip)

Day 5 (Monday, May 18): Northern Sport Hub & Leadership

  • Hotel check-in in Manchester.
  • Visit Manchester United Museum.
  • Participate in a workshop on Equality, Diversity, and Inclusion.

Day 12 (Monday, May 25): Departure
